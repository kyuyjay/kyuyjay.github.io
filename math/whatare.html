Negative Numbers: About

Negative numbers are numbers with a value lower than zero. They can be seen as an extension of the positive numbers, such that the equation x âˆ’ y = z always has a solution no matter what x or y is.

Negative numbers are useful to describe values on a scale that goes below zero, such as temperature, in which during winter in temperate countries the temperature could dip below 0 degress celcius. Also, in keeping shop accounts, negative numbers can be used to represent losses or what people owe.

In the past, when negative numbers were not popularized, shopkeepers had to write numbers in red to represent negative numbers. Negative numbers are also used in sports such as golf, to show scores of players, or in directions, in which there may be bearings such as -15 degress celcius. It is also as in velocity, where there may be a negative velocity, and in engineering, where negative forces are also very important.
