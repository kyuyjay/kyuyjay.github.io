Negative Numbers: The Modernization

Gottfried Leibniz and Kowa Seki were the two main founders of modern algebraic notations.

In 1683, Kowa Seki discovered the determinant, discriminant and Bernoulli numbers, and then 2 years later, he solved the general cubic equation, as well as quartic and quintic equations.

Gottfried Leibniz continued on his research, and 8 years later solved the systems of simultaneous linear equation using matrices and determinants.
